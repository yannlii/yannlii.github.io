overheard

photographs, fragments and what remains

我在毕业论文的致谢中写过：“在每一个不得不原路返回的十字路口，马友友（Yo-Yo Ma）对大提琴始终如一的热情，以及淡豹关于成为一个不被打断的人的想象，一直是我精神世界里无形的支点。” 马友友把大提琴视为一生的使命。我也时常用 find your own cello 这句话提醒自己去寻找那把属于自己的“大提琴”。 于是在 Cello Suite No.1 的单曲循环里，赶在毕业之前完成了这个网站。

如果有一天能继续拓展这些影像，我想在废墟中策划一场展览：把照片悬挂在被遗弃的建筑空地和斑驳的墙面上。参观者经过时，四周安静得近乎震耳，只剩下影像清诉背后的故事。这是 overheard 的由来。

In the acknowledgements of my graduation thesis, I once wrote: “At every crossroads where I had no choice but to turn back, Yo-Yo Ma’s unwavering devotion to the cello, and Dan Bao’s pursuit of becoming a person who is not constantly interrupted, have remained invisible pillars of my inner world.”

With Cello Suite No.1 on repeat, I finished this website just before graduation. Yo-Yo Ma has always regarded the cello as his life’s calling. I, too, kept reminding myself with the phrase find your own cello—to search for that one thing that is truly mine.

恍恍惚惚，又过了三年，网站也生了灰。 这里的大多数照片来自那台被我称作“口袋朋友”的 Olympus Mju II，直到 2023 年底它被我丢在了那不勒斯的海边。

Three years passed in a haze, and the website had gathered dust. Most of these photos were taken with my “pocket friend,” an Olympus u2, which I left on the Naples shore at the end of 2023.
