overheard

photographs, fragments and what remains

2023

我在毕业论文的致谢中写过：“在每一个不得不原路返回的十字路口，马友友（Yo-Yo Ma）对大提琴始终如一的热情，以及淡豹关于成为一个不被打断的人的想象，一直是我精神世界里无形的支点。” 马友友把大提琴视为一生的使命。我也时常用 find your own cello 这句话提醒自己去寻找那把属于自己的“大提琴”。 于是在 Cello Suite No.1 的单曲循环里，赶在毕业之前完成了这个网站。

如果有一天能继续拓展这些影像，我想在废墟中策划一场展览：把照片悬挂在被遗弃的建筑空地和斑驳的墙面上。参观者经过时，四周安静得近乎震耳，只剩下影像清诉背后的故事。这是我把这些胶片档案命名为 overheard 的原因。

In the acknowledgements of my thesis, I once wrote: “At every crossroads where I had no choice but to turn back, Yo-Yo Ma’s unwavering devotion to the cello, and Dan Leopard’s pursuit of becoming a person who is not constantly interrupted, have remained invisible pillars of my inner world.”

With cello 1 on repeat, I finished this website just before graduation. Yo-Yo Ma has always regarded the cello as his life’s calling. I, too, kept reminding myself with the phrase find your own cello—to search for that one thing that is truly mine. At the time, it felt as though the moment was quietly approaching.

If I ever have the chance to work further with these photographs, I imagine an exhibition set among ruins—images displayed on the walls of abandoned lots and weathered buildings. As visitors pass through, the silence would feel almost deafening, leaving only faint murmurs of stories lingering behind the images. That sensation is why I named this site overheard.

2026

恍恍惚惚，又过了三年，网站也生了灰。 这里的大多数照片来自那台被我称作“口袋朋友”的 Olympus u2。直到 2023 年底，它被我留在了那不勒斯的海边——像是陪我一起发完了一场呆。

爷爷去世之后，我几乎不再拍胶片。每次想整理已经洗好的底片，都不知道会在哪一卷里突然翻到和他一起逛公园的照片，于是又悄悄退回原位。

在津巴布韦的新年夜，我独自坐在阳台上赏月。忽然想起苏轼写过的那句：

“料得年年肠断处，明月夜，短松冈。”

有些思念不需要抵达，它会在同一轮月亮下反复出现。

Three years passed in a haze, and the website had gathered dust. Most of these photos were taken with my “pocket friend,” an Olympus u2, which I left on the Naples shore at the end of 2023, as if it had drifted with me through a long, quiet pause.

After my grandfather passed, I barely touched film. Every time I thought of going through developed rolls, I hesitated—never knowing which one might suddenly reveal a park walk with him—so I put them aside again and again.

On New Year’s Eve in Zimbabwe, I sat alone on the balcony, gazing at the moon. A line from Su Shi came to mind:

“Grief returns year after year, under the bright moon, on Short Pine Hill.”

Some longing doesn’t need to reach anywhere; it simply returns, again and again, beneath the same moon.
