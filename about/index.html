overheard

我在毕业论文的致谢里写过，“在每一个不得不原路返回的十字路口，马友友（Yo-Yo Ma）对大提琴始终如一的热情，以及淡豹关于成为一个不被打断的人的想象，一直是我精神世界里的无形支柱。”

马友友把大提琴视为一生的使命，我也时常用这句话提醒自己去寻找那把属于自己的“大提琴”。于是在Cello Suite No.1的单曲循环里，赶在毕业之前完成了这个网站。

如果继续拍下去，我想把照片挂在街道或公园的树上，或者废弃的建筑空地里。散步的人经过时，四周静谧，只剩照片低语——这是 overheard 的由来。

In the acknowledgements of my graduation thesis, I once wrote, “At every crossroads where I had to turn back, Yo-Yo Ma’s unwavering devotion to the cello, and Dan Bao’s vision of becoming a person undisturbed, have been invisible anchors in my inner world.”

Yo-Yo Ma treats the cello as his life’s calling, and I often remind myself with the phrase find your own cello - to seek the thing that truly belongs to me. With Cello Suite No.1 on endless repeat, this website came out just before graduation.

If I continue taking photos, I would like to hang them on the trees along a quiet street or in a park, or in the empty spaces of abandoned buildings. As people pass by, the world falls silent and only the images whisper their stories. This is how overheard was born.

恍恍惚惚又三年，网站也开始生灰。这里的大多数照片都来自「口袋朋友」 Olympus Mju II，直到 2023 年底我把它丢在了那不勒斯某个发完呆的海边。

爷爷去世之后我几乎不再拍胶片。每次想整理已经洗好的底片，都不知道会在哪一卷里突然翻到和他一起在公园散步的照片，于是一再搁置。

新年夜，我独自坐在津巴布韦新家的阳台上赏月，忽而想起苏轼那句：“料得年年肠断处，明月夜，短松冈。”

有些思念无处抵达，但会在同一轮月亮下反复出现。

Three more years drifted by in a blur, and the website had gathered dust. Most of these photos were taken with my pocket companion - an Olympus Mju II, which I let slip away on a quiet day by the shores of Napoli at the end of 2023.

After my grandfather passed away, I barely touched film. Every time I thought of sorting through the developed rolls, I never knew which one might unexpectedly reveal a walk in the park with him, so I set them aside again and again.

On this New Year’s Eve, I sat alone on the balcony of my new home in Zimbabwe, lost in the gentle gaze of the moon. A line from Su Shi came to mind: “Must it be that every year I'll think of that heart-breaking place, Where the moon shines brightly in the night, and bare pines guard the tomb.”

Some longings never need to reach anywhere; they return again and again under the same moon.
